## Decisions Made

- **tachyonfx added as dev-dependency (auto-fix, Rule 3):** The plan's acceptance criteria require `tachyonfx::fx::fade_from` in the source, and bounded effects must use raw tachyonfx constructors (not the full-grid `effects::` convenience wrappers). Since `happyterminals` had no direct `tachyonfx` dep and Rust does not expose transitive dep crate names, the example could not compile without adding the dep. Scoped to `[dev-dependencies]` so the library crate's dependency surface is unchanged for downstream consumers.
- **Camera + reveal geometry choices** (discretionary per plan):
  - `ROTATION_SPEED = 0.6` rad/s — slow and cinematic, avoids dizzying motion under text.
  - `OrbitCamera { elevation: 0.3, distance: 4.0 }` — keeps bunny roughly in the lower 2/3 of the viewport so the top-anchored title rect doesn't overlap.
  - `title_rect` at `y=4`, width `min(44, grid.width-2)`, 4 rows tall — leaves 3 rows for content (title / tagline / gap / cue).
  - `REVEAL_DURATION_MS = 900` — long enough to read the effect, short enough that pressing Space for a replay feels responsive.

**Human verification deferred to the phase-level manual step** (per the `feedback_visual_iteration` memory — snapshot tests don't substitute for human judgment on hero visuals). When the human runs the example, they should observe:

1. A slowly rotating ASCII bunny (azimuth increases at 0.6 rad/s).
2. Title "HAPPY TERMINALS" (white, bold), tagline "GPU-quality effects on text" (cyan), cue ">  press [space] to continue" (yellow) overlaid near the top center.
3. First ~900ms: text fades in from black to fg (fade_from) — bunny shading does NOT shimmer.

6. Q or Ctrl-C: clean exit.
